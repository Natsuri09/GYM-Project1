Our GYM Equipments

Learn To Use, Apply To Understand!!

Dumbbells

Build muscle in arms, chest, shoulders, back, and legs.

Leg Press Machine

A machine where you push weight away from you using your legs, usually seated or at a 45° angle. It targets major leg muscles without stressing your back like free-weight squats might.

Cycling

Great for cardio, fat burning, and lower-body endurance. Adjustable resistance to simulate flat roads or hill climbs.

Kettlebells

A cannonball-shaped weight with a handle. The off-center mass works your stabilizing muscles harder.

  • Full-body workouts
  • Explosive power & cardio
  • Building grip strength
  • Core and back stability

Treadmill

A machine with a moving belt that lets you walk, jog, or run in place. Adjustable speed and incline simulate hills or flat roads.

Flat Bench

A sturdy, horizontal bench used for various strength exercises — with dumbbells, a barbell, or just bodyweight.

Power Rack

A sturdy metal frame with safety bars. Perform heavy barbell lifts like squats, bench press, overhead press — safely, even without a spotter.

Smith Machine

A barbell fixed on vertical rails, allowing vertical movement only. Includes safety hooks to secure the bar at any point by twisting your wrists.

Multi-Gym Equipment

A compact all-in-one gym system that allows for multiple strength training exercises:

  • Lat pulldowns
  • Chest presses
  • Leg extensions
  • Seated rows
  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep pushdowns
